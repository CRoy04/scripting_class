About

Welcome to the city of Lethbridge, where prairie meets coulee, and in the distance the mountains are seen towering over the horizon. Surrounded by endless fields of canola and wheat. The windy city of Lethbridge is home to the world’s largest viaduct bridge, connecting the Canadian Pacific Railway across the Oldman River Valley. Lethbridge, when established in the late 1800’s as a coalmining community, was originally called Coalbanks. The name was changed to Lethbridge in honour of William Lethbridge, who was an original shareholder in the NCNC (Northwest Coal and Navigation Company.)

Lethbridge Athletic Associations

Lethbridge has a variety of athletic associations, being home to the Lethbridge Bulls from the WCBL (The Western Canadian Baseball League) and the Lethbridge Hurricanes from the WHL (Western Hockey League). As well as many others like the ACAC (Alberta Colleges Athletic Conference) home of the Lethbridge Kodiaks.
